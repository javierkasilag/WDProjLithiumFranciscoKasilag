Toronto Accord

Britain, forced to cut ties with a Europe now under Nazi domination, turned across the Atlantic to America as its closest ally, the two fighting side by side against Japan in Asia along with their Commonwealth allies. The result was the Toronto Accord, a formal alliance built by the remaining powers of the democratic west, standing for collective security, democracy and human rights and as a united front against the threat of Nazi Germany.

Major members:

  • United State of America (Leader)
  • United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland (Leader)
  • Dominion of Canada (Leader)
  • Dominion of Australia
  • Dominion of New Zealand
  • Republic of Ireland
  • Kingdom of Belgium
  • Kingdom of the Netherlands
  • French Authority

    • Neue Ordnung

    Neue Ordnung - The New Order, was never formed as an official faction, it serves as a term for Germany's direct sphere of influence with partners which might be cooperating closely with the Nazi regime. The starting members of the Neue Ordnung were all German controlled puppet governments, which their sole purpose within this unofficial faction is to enrich Metropolitan Germany with each subject's local resources.

    Major members:

  • Greater Germanic Reich (Leader)
  • Kingdom of Denmark
  • Norwegian State
  • Slovak Republic
  • Serbian Government of National Salvation

    • Mediterranean Pact

    Formed around the Italian Empire at the peak of its power after Axis victory in the European Wars, the Mediterranean Pact focuses on maintaining hegemony over the Mediterranean Sea, making the faction more militaristic with romanticised ideals of a Roman Empire at its core. The Mediterranean Pact also serves as a counter balance to Nazi Germany's control over Mainland Europe.

    Major members:

  • Italian Empire (Leader)
  • Kingdom of Albania
  • Independent State of Croatia
  • Hellenic State
  • Governorate of Montenegro
